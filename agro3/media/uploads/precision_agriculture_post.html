üéØ What is Precision Agriculture?
Precision agriculture is a farming management concept that uses technology to observe, measure, and respond to variability in crops and soil conditions. It's about doing the right thing, in the right place, at the right time, with the right amount of inputs.

üå± Variable Rate Technology (VRT)

One of the most powerful applications of precision agriculture is Variable Rate Technology, which allows farmers to apply different amounts of inputs across their fields based on specific needs of each area.

Variable Rate Fertilization: Applying precise amounts of nutrients based on soil test results
Precision Seeding: Optimal seed placement and spacing for maximum germination rates
Aerial Monitoring: Drones equipped with multispectral cameras detect crop stress early
